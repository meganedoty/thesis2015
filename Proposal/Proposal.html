Practitioners

Meg Lewis

Meg Lewis is a print and web designer who works as a part of the freelance studio Ghostly Ferns. Her work on the web has a friendly and welcoming feel that nevertheless also has an air modern, fun, dynamic and perhaps even empowering. She also appears to be a really super human being.

Frank Chimero

Frank Chimero has worked as an illustrator, designer and author. His illustrated articles on the web as well as his book, The Shape of Design, are both persuasive and thought provokingly sharp in content, leading to new ideas and insights. His thoughts on designing for print and the web at once (The Great Discontent redesign, for example).

Katie Kovalcin

I first came across Katie Kovalcin from her talk at Smashing Conference NYC 2015 where she spoke about closing the gap between front-end designers and developers. The aesthetic she is able to communicate in her work is also fresh and playful in their use of modern web programming capabilities.

Philippe Apeloig

A designer primarily in print, Philippe Apeloig works with type and grids in to create strong visual impact in really cunning and smart ways. The work he produces is bold, memorable and elegant.

Meagan Fisher

Another (female!) web designer / front-end dev whose work I've found through volunteering. She had spoken at Smashing Conference NYC 2014.
