Working project title : Épanouir

Project Description

Studying abroad can be nerve wracking. Especially if one has mental health quarries. The internet is bursting with travel resources and blogs, though usually portraying the experience en rose. The internet is also plentiful with mental health resources, though usually grossly sympathetic in design. Combining these results in a strange mess of confusion. I’m looking to create a platform to fill in the gap: someplace one can find resources free from the sad stigma of mental health help. This may also be a place motivating users to check in — whether it’s been a phenomenal day, or just kind of not great! Because sometimes people simply need to share things, get them out and keep moving. It can also be great to reflect and see how far one has come. Investigating UX/UI, defining voice and style, and using web technologies will result in such a platform that accepts anxiety, depression, stress, and other mental health qualms as normal roadblocks that overcoming is nevertheless attainable.

Topic Background

The stigma behind mental health creates a force where it almost feels shameful to admit struggle. The goal with this thesis project would not be to eliminate stigma completely, but to acknowledge that it is a reason why numbers of students would go abroad without giving this information and thus not be given information that could help. It would fill in a gap where resources dealing with such a situation are difficult to find, and would work to provide a sense of normalcy.

Questions

  1. How can mental health information presented in a non-sympathetic manner and a matter-of-fact way alter stigma?
  2. Can access to resources on traveling with mental health quarries outside of a university center abroad office ameliorate one's experience abroad?
  3. Would students be able to encourage and interact with each other concerning problems and triumphs abroad?
  4. Are there currently any resources for students going abroad who struggle with mental illness?

Environmental Scan

helpguide.org

Resources on an array of health points. Their site uses soft colors, natural, sympathetic, first person, clean / sanitary, retirement home, and concerning imagery. The aesthetic used does not leave the user at ease or confident in their ability to get past any difficulties.

MIUSA — Mobility International USA

Extensive resource section. Hard / crisp aesthetic. Can filter by type of diability, location. Stories highlighting successes often written after the writer has returned to their home country. Adds an accessible PDF at the end of each article.

Experience project

The site is clean in design and appears to be targeted aesthetically to young adults and university students. Users can create groups or make small posts similar to tweets that other users may reply to. The idea is there, but moderation is lacking; the result is a lot of irrelevant material better suited for other social media platforms.

Misc forums

Vicissitudes of forums regarding both study abroad and mental health exist. These typically use a generic structure (no need to reinvent the wheel) for creating threads and replies. Straightforward, to the point, and personalized answers.

University international programs website

It appears that most universities have some sort of site set up for their international programs. The one set up for SUNY New Paltz gives general information about a program, accessible to the general public. After registering as a user one gains access to more materials as relevant to the program they are applying for. Only after being accepted to a program do informational documents on health and safety abroad become available.

Potential Outcomes

This project will adress the issue of a lack of resources pertaining to mental health quarries and study abroad. It will present information in a normalized manner and be freely accessible. During the design and development process, students at SUNY New Paltz will be the primary audience. Initial functionality includes building a resource hub while designing for scalability, and the intention to add registered users later on. The project will primarily focus on the web, but may also include a poster series to and social media materials as promotion.

Methods

This project will use web technlogies to take form. This includes standard HTML / CSS markup, exploring SASS as a pre-processing language for writing CSS, jQuery and Javascript for interaction, some PHP, and using Bootstrap as a framework. Posters and social media materials will also be created within the Adobe Creative Suite. The Center for International Programs and the Health Center will be consulted for assistance on putting together information. Technical skills to learn include implementing user registration later on, which would detail saving information as well as generating content.

Annotated Bibliography

Nicely Said Non-design

Nicole Fenton, Kate Kiefer Lee

Published in 2015, this book gives discusses writing content for the web where style, vocabulary, and situation based appropriateness is concerned. Writing content for Épanouir, vocabulary and tone on panic attacks would differ from a page for someone in the midst of experienceing a panic attack trying to come down.

Over-stressed, Overwhelmed, and Over Here: Resident Directors and the Challenges of Student Mental Health Abroad Non-design

John Lucas

Published in Frontiers: The Interdisciplinary Journal of Study Abroad, this article discusses study abroad through the point of view of a program coordinator. John Lucas includes details on how he has handled certain cases handling physical and mental health with students studying abroad.

International Students' Stratageies for Well-Being Non-Design

Wen-Chih Tseng, Fred B. Newton

This article discusses experience of international students studying abroad to work on their skill in using the english language. Topics address well-being, coping skills, cultural adjustment, and qualitiative research on how several students were able to derive a positive experience from studying abroad.

Mental Health and Study Abroad: Responding to the Concern Non-design

Les McCabe

While many students do not divulge information regarding mental health where there exists pre-existing problems on forms, this article details how students who properly plan are able to successfully study abroad.

Smarter Than You Think: How Technology is Changing Our Minds for the Better Non-design

Clive Thompson

Modern technological capabilites allow for changes within the classroom with regards to motivation and effort, as well as allow for people internationally to participate in humanitarian efforts. Technology that exists in our lives allows for collaboration to improve results and access.
