Questions

  1. How can mental health information presented in a non-sympathetic manner and a matter-of-fact way alter stigma?
  2. Can access to resources on traveling with mental health quarries outside of a university center abroad office ameliorate one's experience abroad?
  3. Would students be able to encourage and interact with each other concerning problems and triumphs abroad?
  4. Are there currently any resources for students going abroad who struggle with mental illness?

Practitioners

Meg Lewis

Meg Lewis is a print and web designer who works as a part of the freelance studio Ghostly Ferns. Her work on the web has a friendly and welcoming feel that nevertheless also has an air modern, fun, dynamic and perhaps even empowering. She also appears to be a really super human being.

Frank Chimero

Frank Chimero has worked as an illustrator, designer and author. His illustrated articles on the web as well as his book, The Shape of Design, are both persuasive and thought provokingly sharp in content, leading to new ideas and insights. His thoughts on designing for print and the web at once (The Great Discontent redesign, for example).

Katie Kovalcin

I first came across Katie Kovalcin from her talk at Smashing Conference NYC 2015 where she spoke about closing the gap between front-end designers and developers. The aesthetic she is able to communicate in her work is also fresh and playful in their use of modern web programming capabilities.

Philippe Apeloig

A designer primarily in print, Philippe Apeloig works with type and grids in to create strong visual impact in really cunning and smart ways. The work he produces is bold, memorable and elegant.

Meagan Fisher

Another (female!) web designer / front-end dev whose work I've found through volunteering. She had spoken at Smashing Conference NYC 2014.

Annotated Bibliography

Nicely Said Non-design

Nicole Fenton, Kate Kiefer Lee

Published in 2015, this book gives discusses writing content for the web where style, vocabulary, and situation based appropriateness is concerned. Writing content for Épanouir, vocabulary and tone on panic attacks would differ from a page for someone in the midst of experienceing a panic attack trying to come down.

Over-stressed, Overwhelmed, and Over Here: Resident Directors and the Challenges of Student Mental Health Abroad Non-design

John Lucas

Published in Frontiers: The Interdisciplinary Journal of Study Abroad, this article discusses study abroad through the point of view of a program coordinator. John Lucas includes details on how he has handled certain cases handling physical and mental health with students studying abroad.

International Students' Stratageies for Well-Being Non-Design

Wen-Chih Tseng, Fred B. Newton

The Mobile Book Design / technical

Edited by Smashing Magazine

Consists of a selection of writings from an array of web designers and front-end web developers. The selection of topics covered here are diverse, including wireframing, optimization, responsive web design, and even designing for gestures on the web.

Javascript & jQuery Design / technical

By Jon Duckett

A resource on writing Javascript & jQuery. Codecademy is great, as is w3 schools, but this book is a beautifully designed resource that will prove useful in adding interactivity to code, or later on using a javascript library to maintain user state.

Designing for Emotion Design / technical

Aaron Walter

A UX designer at Mail Chimp, Aaron Walter goes over the principles of user experience design, figuring out emotional factors into building a website. Including defining a delightful aesthetic, all the while maintaining usability and functionality.

The Shape of Design Design / technical

Frank Chimero

Frank Chimero's first self-published book is full of insights applicable to the goals of design overall. His writing speaks to the soul of design rather than the technical aspects of either print or the web, though his other writings can be accessed on the web for thoughts regarding this.

On Web Typography Design / technical

Jason Santa Maria

Type on the web has come a long way in recent years. Published by A Book Apart, On Web Typography goes over practices, principles, and reasoning in writing code for good type on the web. The book is written from a technical point of view, allowing a mélange of prior design knowledge to balance with logical reasoning.
